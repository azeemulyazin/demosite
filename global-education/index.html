ASK YOUR QUESTIONS
1. Why should I choose Russia, Uzbekistan, Moldova, or Georgia for my studies?
These countries are world-renowned for providing high-quality education, particularly in Medicine (MBBS) and Engineering, at a fraction of the cost of Indian private colleges. All universities we represent are NMC (National Medical Commission) and WHO recognized, ensuring your degree is valid globally and in India.
2. Is there an entrance exam for admission in these countries?
For most universities in these regions, there is no separate entrance exam. Admission is primarily based on your 12th-grade marks. However, for Indian students pursuing Medicine, qualifying for the NEET exam is mandatory to practice in India later.
3. Will I face a language barrier during my course?
Not at all. The programs we suggest are fully English-medium for international students. While you will be taught the local language (like Russian or Georgian) as a subject to help you interact with patients during clinical rotations and in daily life, your core academic curriculum remains in English.
4. Does Global Education Consultation have offices outside of Kerala?
Yes! To better serve our growing student community, we have a strong presence across South India. You can visit our expert counselors at our branches in Bangalore, Mysore, Mangalore, and Coimbatore for personalized, face-to-face guidance and document processing.
5. What is the total cost of living and tuition in these regions?
These are some of the most budget-friendly destinations in the world. Total tuition fees typically range from ₹15 Lakhs to ₹30 Lakhs for the entire 6-year course, and the monthly cost of living (including food and hostel) is often as low as ₹10,000 to ₹15,000, which is comparable to living in a major Indian city.
6. How do you assist students after they leave India?
Our support is "Land-to-Launch." We don't just stop at the visa; we provide on-ground assistance in Russia, Uzbekistan, Moldova, and Georgia. This includes airport pickup, hostel allocation, local SIM card registration, and even assistance with the FMGE/NExT coaching for medical students.
